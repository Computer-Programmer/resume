Or, does your child/teen play lots of games,
Can't leave a device,
Can't live without a device?

Then your child/teen may have digital addiction



These young addicts include 3-year-olds who scream when they can't have their tablets, middle-schoolers whose grades nosedive because they can't quit texting or posting on Facebook, and high-schoolers who compulsively play online games.



It makes people think: Digital Addiction is becoming a big problem!

Digital Addiction's effect is metabolic syndrome, the first step to diabetes!

If you said yes to any of those questions, you might want to have some ideas:


1. Write up a schedule specifying how much screen time your child can have each day and give consequences for breaking the rules.
2. Make a rule that during homework time, at dinner, and one hour before bedtime, all electronic devices get shut off. That means yours, too.
3. Use parental controls to limit your child's cell phone, TV, computer time, and other electronics.
4. Find out what's going on in your child's life. Kids who are stressed or depressed are more likely to develop a screen addiction.
5. Tell your child to spend at least one hour each day playing outside.
6. Get your child involved in volunteer work, sports or other community activities.

You should set a good example for your child and follow these rules.


Digital Addiction is becoming a problem! Please try to prevent it.
